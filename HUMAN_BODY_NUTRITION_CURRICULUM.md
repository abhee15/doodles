# Human Body & Nutrition Curriculum

## Vision: Body Systems → Food Science → Healthy Habits

**Status**: Planning Phase
**Target**: 12-14 new Science games covering human body systems and nutrition science
**Approach**: Story-based learning with interactive 3D anatomy, animations, lab experiments, and food interactions

---

## Part 1: The Human Body (6-7 games)

### A. Digestive System Journey (Game 1)

**ID**: `digestion-explorer`
**Tagline**: Follow your food on an epic adventure through the digestive system!

**Core Story**: A piece of pizza (or apple) travels through the digestive tract with a fun narrator

- Mouth → Esophagus → Stomach → Small Intestine → Large Intestine
- Each stop is a dramatic scene showing what happens to food

**Visualization Techniques**:

1. **Interactive 3D Scene** (Three.js): Cross-section of digestive organs
   - Draggable food item that moves through the system
   - Clickable organs to learn function
2. **Animated Transformations**: Show food breaking down at each stage
   - Mouth: Chewing animation with teeth
   - Stomach: Churning animation with acid bubbles
   - Small Intestine: Nutrient absorption glow effects
3. **Glossary Cards**:
   - Saliva: Breaks down starch (enzyme animation)
   - Stomach acid: Breaks down proteins
   - Bile: Helps digest fats
   - Nutrients: Absorbed through intestine walls

**Lab Section**:

- "Simulate Digestion": Drag food through each stage, select which nutrients get absorbed
- Timer-based progression showing how long digestion takes (3-5 hours)

**Memory Hook**: "STOMACH JOURNEY" acronym or song

---

### B. Circulatory System (Game 2)

**ID**: `heart-flow-quest`
**Tagline**: Follow a blood cell's journey around the entire body in 60 seconds!

**Core Story**: You ARE a red blood cell named "Oxygen" traveling through arteries and veins

**Visualization Techniques**:

1. **Interactive 3D Body**: Full torso showing heart, lungs, major arteries/veins
   - Pulsing heart animation
   - Blood flowing through vessels with particle effects
   - Color change: Blue (deoxygenated) → Red (oxygenated) as it passes lungs
2. **Journey Map** (2D):
   - Heart → Lungs (pick up oxygen) → Heart → Body (deliver oxygen) → Back to heart
   - Click on each organ to see what happens
3. **Glossary**:
   - Heart: The pump
   - Arteries: Carry oxygenated blood away
   - Veins: Return deoxygenated blood
   - Lungs: Trade CO2 for oxygen

**Interactive Lab**:

- "Speed Challenge": Keep blood flowing smoothly (avoid blockages)
- "Pulse Check": Count heartbeats per minute across different activities (rest, exercise, stress)

---

### C. Respiratory System (Game 3)

**ID**: `breath-deep-explorer`
**Tagline**: Discover how your lungs turn air into life!

**Core Story**: Follow an oxygen molecule from air to your bloodstream

**Visualization Techniques**:

1. **Lungs 3D Model**: Animated lungs inflating/deflating
   - Cross-section showing alveoli (tiny air sacs)
   - Oxygen molecules moving across alveoli wall into blood
   - CO2 molecules leaving blood and being exhaled
2. **Comparative Animation**:
   - Shallow breathing vs. deep breathing (different O2 absorption)
   - What happens during exercise (faster breathing, more oxygen)
   - What happens during sleep (slower breathing)
3. **Interactive 3D Breathing**: User controls breath depth/speed with slider
   - See oxygen saturation change in real-time

**Glossary**:

- Trachea: Windpipe
- Bronchi: Branch into lungs
- Alveoli: Tiny air sacs where gas exchange happens
- Diaphragm: Muscle that controls breathing

---

### D. Brain & Nervous System (Game 4)

**ID**: `brain-command-center`
**Tagline**: How does your brain control everything? Discover the nervous system!

**Core Story**: A "neural signal" (electrical impulse) travels from brain → spinal cord → muscles, showing how you think, feel, and move

**Visualization Techniques**:

1. **3D Brain Model** with labeled regions:
   - Cerebrum (thinking, memory)
   - Cerebellum (balance, coordination)
   - Brain stem (breathing, heartbeat)
   - Animated neurons firing (lightning-like effects)
2. **Reflex Arc Animation**:
   - Touch hot surface → signal travels to spinal cord → signal bounces back → hand pulls away
   - Show parallel pathway to brain (you feel pain after pulling away)
3. **Five Senses Visualization**:
   - Click different senses to see signal pathways:
   - Eyes → Visual cortex
   - Ears → Auditory cortex
   - Nose → Smell center
   - Tongue → Taste center
   - Touch → Sensory cortex

**Lab Section**:

- "Reaction Speed Test": Click as fast as possible to measure nerve signal speed
- "Memory Game": Test different brain regions' functions

---

### E. Skeletal & Muscular System (Game 5)

**ID**: `bones-muscles-builder`
**Tagline**: Build your skeleton and learn how muscles make you move!

**Core Story**: A character doing different movements (running, jumping, reaching) with internal anatomy visible

**Visualization Techniques**:

1. **3D Skeleton Model**:
   - All major bones labeled
   - Toggle to show muscle overlays
   - Click on a bone/muscle to learn its name and function
2. **Movement Animation**:
   - Show how muscles contract (get shorter)
   - Opposite muscles working together (biceps/triceps)
   - Joints bending and moving
   - Show these at different body parts (knee, elbow, shoulder, hip)
3. **Bone Strength Layer**:
   - Show calcium and minerals in bones
   - How exercise strengthens bones
   - Growth plates in children's bones

**Glossary**:

- Skeleton: 206 bones supporting the body
- Joints: Places where bones meet
- Tendons: Connect muscles to bones
- Ligaments: Connect bones to bones
- Cartilage: Cushions joints

**Interactive Lab**:

- "Muscle Works": Pair each muscle with its movement
- "Build Strength": Show what happens to muscles with exercise over time

---

### F. Immune System (Game 6)

**ID**: `immune-warriors`
**Tagline**: Discover the tiny defenders protecting you from germs!

**Core Story**: White blood cells as "warriors" fighting invading bacteria and viruses

**Visualization Techniques**:

1. **Microscopic Battle Scene**:
   - White blood cells (animated) chasing down pathogens
   - Different types of white cells with unique abilities
   - Antibodies as "shields" coating pathogens
2. **Lymph Node Activation**:
   - When germs enter → lymph nodes swell
   - White cells multiply and prepare
   - Fever animation (body heating to kill germs)
3. **Vaccination Story**:
   - Show weak/dead pathogen entering
   - Body "learns" to recognize it
   - Next time it enters, immune system is ready

**Glossary**:

- White blood cells: Fighter cells
- Antibodies: Protein shields
- Lymph nodes: Immune system control centers
- Fever: Body's defense temperature
- Vaccine: Training exercise for immune system

### G. Endocrine System (Optional, Game 7)

**ID**: `hormone-controllers`
**Tagline**: Tiny glands, powerful messages — meet your hormone system!

**Core Story**: Different glands producing hormones that control growth, mood, energy, and more

**Visualization Techniques**:

## Part 2: Food & Nutrition (5-7 games)

### H. Food Groups & Macronutrients (Game 8)

**ID**: `nutrition-quest`
**Tagline**: Every food has a superpower — discover what your body needs!

**Core Story**: Different foods with "power cards" showing what nutrients they contain and what superpowers they unlock

**Visualization Techniques**:

1. **Food Pyramid Interactive**:
   - Draggable food items to different groups
   - Show quantities recommended per day
   - Visual proportions (visual learning for kids)
2. **Nutrient Breakdown Cards**:
   - Tap any food to see: Calories, Protein, Fat, Carbs, Fiber, Vitamins
   - Visual bars showing nutrient amounts
   - Emoji icons for each nutrient type
3. **Body Function Links**:
   - Protein → Muscles & Growth
   - Carbs → Energy & Brain Power
   - Fat → Cell membranes & Brain
   - Fiber → Digestion & Fullness
   - Vitamins → Various superpowers

**Lab Section**:

- "Build a Meal": Drag foods onto a plate to create balanced nutrition
- "Energy Calculator": Different foods = different energy levels; see impact on activities

### I. Vitamins & Minerals (Game 9)

**ID**: `vitamin-adventure`
**Tagline**: Tiny nutrients with big jobs — meet the vitamin superheroes!

**Core Story**: Each vitamin/mineral is a "superhero" with unique powers and food sources

**Visualization Techniques**:

1. **Vitamin Character Cards**:
   - Vitamin C: Orange superhero (fights infections, heals wounds)
   - Vitamin D: Sunshine hero (builds bones, mood)
   - Vitamin A: Eye sight hero (sees in the dark)
   - Calcium: Bone builder (strong skeleton)
   - Iron: Energy carrier (red blood cells)
   - Show each with:
     - Superhero costume/design
     - Food sources (with pictures)
     - What job it does in body
2. **Body System Spotlight**:
   - Bones need: Calcium, Vitamin D, Magnesium
   - Muscles need: Protein, Iron, B vitamins
   - Brain needs: Omega-3, Choline, B vitamins
   - Immune system needs: Vitamin C, Zinc, Vitamin A
3. **Deficiency Stories**:
   - What happens if you don't get enough? (tired, weak bones, slow healing, etc.)

**Interactive Lab**:

- "Balanced Diet Challenge": Ensure you get all necessary vitamins throughout a week
- "Rainbow Plate": Eating different colored foods ensures you get different vitamins

### J. Energy & Calories (Game 10)

**ID**: `calorie-engine`
**Tagline**: Food = Fuel! Watch how your body burns energy!

**Core Story**: Calories as "fuel units" that power different activities

**Visualization Techniques**:

1. **Body as Engine**:
   - Fuel tank showing daily caloric needs
   - Different food portions = different fuel amounts
   - Show which foods are "dense fuel" vs "light fuel"
2. **Activity Comparisons**:
   - Running costs 500 kcal/hour (show animation)
   - Walking costs 300 kcal/hour
   - Sleeping costs 50 kcal/hour
   - Playing costs 400 kcal/hour
   - Show real-time burning as they run/jump
3. **Basal Metabolic Rate**:
   - Even at rest, body needs fuel to:
     - Keep heart beating
     - Keep lungs breathing
     - Maintain body temperature
     - Keep brain thinking
4. **Age & Growth Impact**:
   - Growing kids need MORE calories than adults (per pound of body weight)
   - Different ages, different needs
   - Show caloric needs for different age groups

**Lab Section**:

- "Energy Tracker": Pick a day's meals and activities, see if energy matches needs
- "Fuel Up For Activity": Choose foods to have enough energy for an event

4. **Protein Sources** (Variety matters):

   - Plant proteins: Beans, nuts, seeds, tofu

5. **Oils & Healthy Fats**:
   - Saturated vs. unsaturated fats
   - Omega-3 fatty acids (fish, nuts)
   - Benefits vs. excess

**Lab Section**:

- "Farm to Plate": Track food from growth to eating

### L. Food Combinations & Digestion (Game 12)

**ID**: `food-pairing-master`
**Tagline**: Some foods work together! Learn which combinations boost nutrition!

**Core Story**: Pair foods together to unlock "nutrient synergies"

**Visualization Techniques**:

1. **Food Pair Multipliers**:
   - Tomato + Olive Oil: Lycopene absorption increases 4x
   - Iron-rich food + Vitamin C: Iron absorption increases 3x
   - Fat + Fat-soluble vitamins: Proper absorption
   - Whole grains + Legumes: Complete protein together
   - Show visual "energy boost" when correct pairing is made
2. **Complete Protein Building**:
   - Rice + Beans = Complete protein
   - Peanut butter + Whole wheat = Complete protein
   - Show amino acid profiles visually
3. **Absorption Animations**:
   - Show nutrients being absorbed better with correct pairings
   - Timing matters: Some nutrients need fat, some need time, some need fiber
4. **Meal Planning**:
   - Build a breakfast/lunch/dinner that's nutritionally complete
   - Show nutrient totals at the end

**Lab Section**:

- "Synergy Score": Drag foods to a plate, calculate total nutrient value
- "Challenge Meals": Given a nutrient target, find foods that hit the goals

## Part 3: Health Habits & Integration (Optional tier, Games 13-14)

### M. Movement & Exercise (Game 13)

**ID**: `exercise-energy`
**Tagline**: Move your body and see instant changes in your muscles, heart, and brain!

**Core Story**: Real-time body changes as you do different exercises

**Visualization Techniques**:

1. **Live Biometrics**:
   - Heart rate increasing with activity
   - Breathing rate changes
   - Muscle engagement showing in real-time
   - Sweat animation
2. **Different Exercise Types**:
   - Cardio: Heart rate up, oxygen uptake up, calorie burn high
   - Strength: Muscles engage and grow, heart moderate
   - Stretching: Flexibility improves, muscle tension releases
   - Balance: Brain coordination center activates
3. **Long-term Changes** (Timeline):
   - Day 1: Muscles sore (lactic acid animation)
   - Week 1: Muscles adapt, strength increases
   - Month 1: Visible muscle growth, endurance improves
   - Year 1: Significant fitness improvements

**Lab Section**:

- "Fitness Tracker": Count how many activities equal one food item's calories
- "Challenge Week": Design a weekly exercise routine and track progress

---

### N. Sleep & Recovery (Game 14)

**ID**: `sleep-science`
**Tagline**: What happens while you sleep? Discover the magic of rest!

**Core Story**: Time-lapse of nighttime body processes and brain consolidation

**Visualization Techniques**:

1. **Brain During Sleep**:
   - Memory consolidation: Memories being "saved" to long-term storage
   - Brain wave patterns changing (light sleep → deep sleep → REM)
   - Glymphatic system clearing toxins (glowing particles flowing)
2. **Body Recovery**:
   - Muscles rebuilding during sleep (if you exercised)
   - Growth hormone release (especially during deep sleep)
   - Immune system strengthening
   - Digestive system resting
3. **Sleep Stages**:
   - Light sleep (Stage 1-2): Slow transition
   - Deep sleep (Stage 3): Restorative, growth happens
   - REM sleep: Dreams, brain consolidation
   - Show 90-minute sleep cycles
4. **Sleep Deprivation Effects**:
   - Less focus, slower reactions (brain graying out)
   - Weakened immunity
   - Mood changes
   - Weight gain connection (hunger hormones)

**Lab Section**:

- "Sleep Schedule Builder": Design an ideal sleep routine for different ages
- "Sleep Quality": Choose habits that improve sleep (exercise timing, screen time, temperature)

## Visualization Strategy Framework

### For ALL Body/Nutrition Games:

1. **3D Anatomy Models**
   - Use Three.js for interactive 3D body parts
   - Clickable organs/structures with highlight/glow
   - Smooth animations showing movement
   - Cross-sections for internal structures
   - Scalable from zoomed-in cellular to full-body view

2. **Animated Transformations**
   - Food breaking down (digestive)
   - Cells multiplying (growth)
   - Particles flowing (blood, hormones)
   - Wave patterns (electrical signals)
   - Color changes (oxygen states, temperature)

3. **Glossary with Icons**
   - Each term has a unique emoji/icon
   - Color-coded by system (red for blood, blue for water, yellow for energy)
   - Expandable definitions with simple language
   - "Simple explanation" vs "detailed explanation"

4. **Storytelling Elements**
   - Named characters following journeys
   - Dramatic moments (food entering stomach with splash animation)
   - Humor where appropriate (food as "passengers," white cells as "warriors")
   - Progress indicators (showing location on journey)

5. **Interactive Lab Experiments**
   - Sliders to control variables
   - Drag-and-drop for building/pairing
   - Real-time visual feedback
   - Gamified challenges with scores

6. **Comparative Visualizations**
   - Healthy vs. unhealthy states side-by-side
   - Before/after animations
   - Activity comparison (rest vs. exercise)
   - Age-based changes (child vs. adult vs. elderly)

## Design Consistency Notes

All games follow **Science Story Framework** established in Phase 1:

- **Archetype**: DOM-based (like Body Map) with 3D embeds where needed
- **Color Scheme**: Science category green (#2E7D32) with per-game accent colors
- **Navigation**: Portal → Game Landing → Scene Selection → Interactive Scene
- **Structure**: Scenes → Lab → Glossary
- **Devices**: Responsive (mobile, tablet, desktop)
